The titles (or section headings) in a resume are critical for organizing content logically and ensuring that both ATS systems and human recruiters can easily navigate the document. Below is a list of **essential titles** that should be included in most resumes, along with optional sections that can enhance your profile depending on your industry, experience, and target role.

---

**Essential Titles**
1. **Contact Information**  
   - Include your full name, phone number, email address, LinkedIn profile (if applicable), and location (city/state or country).  
   - Example:  
     ```
     John Doe  
     Phone: (123) 456-7890 | Email: john.doe@example.com  
     LinkedIn: linkedin.com/in/johndoe | Location: New York, NY
     ```

2. **Professional Summary (or Career Objective)**  
   - A brief 2–4 sentence overview of your professional background, key skills, and career goals tailored to the job description.  
   - Example:  
     ```
     Professional Summary  
     Results-driven marketing professional with 8+ years of experience in digital campaigns, brand strategy, and data-driven decision-making. Proven track record of increasing ROI by 30% through innovative strategies. Seeking to leverage expertise in a leadership role at [Company Name].
     ```

3. **Skills**  
   - Highlight both technical and soft skills relevant to the job. Use bullet points or a table format for clarity.  
   - Example:  
     ```
     Skills  
     Technical: SEO, Google Analytics, HubSpot, WordPress  
     Soft Skills: Leadership, Communication, Problem-Solving  
     ```

4. **Professional Experience (or Work History)**  
   - List your work history in reverse chronological order (most recent first). Include job title, company name, location, and dates of employment. Use bullet points to describe achievements and responsibilities.  
   - Example:  
     ```
     Professional Experience  
     Marketing Manager | ABC Corporation | New York, NY | Jan 2020 – Present  
     - Increased website traffic by 40% through targeted SEO campaigns.  
     - Led a team of 5 to launch 3 successful product launches, generating $1M+ in revenue.  
     Digital Marketing Specialist | XYZ Inc. | San Francisco, CA | Jun 2017 – Dec 2019  
     - Managed social media accounts, growing followers by 25% YoY.  
     ```

5. **Education**  
   - Include degrees earned, institutions attended, graduation dates, and any honors or distinctions.  
   - Example:  
     ```
     Education  
     Bachelor of Science in Marketing | University of California, Los Angeles | 2016  
     GPA: 3.8/4.0 | Dean’s List Honoree  
     ```

6. **Certifications (Optional but Recommended)**  
   - List certifications relevant to the job. Include the issuing organization and date earned (if applicable).  
   - Example:  
     ```
     Certifications  
     Google Analytics Certified | Google | 2022  
     HubSpot Content Marketing Certification | HubSpot | 2021  
     ```

---

### **Optional Titles (Based on Role/Industry)**
1. **Projects**  
   - Highlight key projects that demonstrate your skills and accomplishments, especially if you’re in tech, design, or creative fields.  
   - Example:  
     ```
     Projects  
     E-commerce Website Redesign | Freelance | 2023  
     - Designed and launched a new Shopify store, increasing conversions by 20%.  
     Data Analysis Dashboard | ABC Corp | 2022  
     - Built a Tableau dashboard to visualize sales trends, reducing reporting time by 50%.  
     ```

2. **Awards & Achievements**  
   - Showcase notable recognitions, awards, or accomplishments.  
   - Example:  
     ```
     Awards & Achievements  
     Employee of the Year | XYZ Inc | 2021  
     Published Author | Marketing Insights Journal | 2020  
     ```

     ```

6. **Publications (for Academic or Research Roles)**  
   - List articles, papers, or books you’ve authored.  
   - Example:  
     ```
     Publications  
     “The Future of AI in Marketing” | Journal of Digital Innovation | 2022  
     ```


---

### **Tips for Title Formatting**
- Use **clear, bold headings** for each section to make them stand out.  
- Keep titles concise and professional (e.g., "Experience" instead of "My Work History").  
- Avoid overly creative titles unless applying to creative fields (e.g., "Career Journey" may not resonate with recruiters).  
- Ensure consistency in formatting across all sections.
